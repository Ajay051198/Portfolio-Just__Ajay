My Story so Far - "A wanna be AI engineer"

I finished my schooling in Chennai, India. At this point of my life I did not have a narrow objective towards my career. I was a admirer of technology from as far back as I can remember and this made one thing clear I wanted to work in the tech industry. Having heard about the Mechatronics Degree @ University of Manchester from one of my seniors I had decided that this is what I wanted to do. To be honest I did not know much about it or what exactly Mechatronics people do (as most people don't), but simply made the choice as it was an multidisciplinary course, sounded cool and had a lot to do with robots.

I take pride in a quality of mine that is to grind to achieve something I have set my mind to. In other words I made it to University of Manchester. After I arrive at Manchester, UK a huge cultural shift catches me off guard and things were not as awesome and enjoyable as I remembered seeing in British and American sitcoms. I come to terms with reality, having lost focus in building a career (I mean I was young ..19) I pickup competitive gaming, rise through the ranks and play for 4 different teams. I knew it was not going to take me anywhere and I cannot let my academics be effected in any manner. I continue to score a first in all my courses (still with no idea what I want to do).

My second year at UoM ends, time comes for me to find an summer internship and I start to face the sad reality of every International student in the UK. The huge bias in employment of locals over International student due to strict policies the companies must abide to while employing International students (I'm not 100% on this). I send out 10-15 applications and receive nothing but rejections. I fly back to India and start coming under the pressure of what I am going to do after graduation. (You basically get 4 months after graduation to find a job, if not you are kicked out of the country). Given the statistics of my colleagues I was convinced that it is most probable that I am not going to find a job and I need a backup plan. I decide to continue doing a masters but not in the UK as it would not count as a second degree and/or improve my chances of employment in the UK.

I start looking at Master Degree in other countries which could create a situation with a better chance for me to get employed. I find Mechatronics Design at UBC, I set my goal, I apply and I get accepted.

I make it to UBC, my new journey starts which I believe will change my life and make me infinitely more happier (going on the concept that I have experience on how to deal with a new place and I am going to have a much better start here ). I carry on with my course, things go south and all my expectations remain as expectations. During this not so bright moment of my life, my personal life crashes due to some poor decisions I made in the past and I am left alone in a country I am new in.

Days go by, Weeks go by. I finally decide to find myself, what I really want and who I want to be. To help with this process I start reading, I start meditating, I start running and working out, I start journaling. During some point I was exposed to Data Science and I was fascinated my how powerful data can be. I started learning more about Data Science and Machine learning concepts.

When I educated myself how Deep Learning (Thanks to amazing people like Andrew Ng from [DeepLearning.ai and Jeremy Howard from fast.ai) has revolutionized Machine Learning in the last 5 years and growing ever so fast I was convinced this is what I want to do. That brings it to today. I am still learning DL concepts. My projects and qualifications are listed somewhere on this page.

I am extremely grateful for the sequence and timing of major events in my life, all my experiences good and bad brought so much more clarity in my life. Before November 2019, I chased instant gratification just wanted to get employed somewhere with a good pay and wined about the little problems I had. Now I enjoy the knowledge absorption process and willing to grind for the long run knowing that no good effort is going to waste, also to add now I know what exactly I want to do. I am a "wanna be AI engineer".
